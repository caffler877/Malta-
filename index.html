I. Overview

Malta is a Mediterranean archipelago consisting of three main islands: Malta, Gozo, and Comino. With over 7,000 years of history, the islands offer a unique blend of cultures, ancient sites, and natural beauty. The official languages are Maltese and English, making it easy for English-speaking tourists to navigate. Malta's strategic location has made it a crossroads of civilizations, influenced by the Phoenicians, Romans, Arabs, Normans, Knights of St. John, French, and British. This rich history is reflected in the architecture, cuisine, and culture of the islands.

II. Key Locations and Activities

Valletta (Capital City)

  • St. John's Co-Cathedral: Baroque masterpiece with Caravaggio paintings
    • Admission: €15 for adults
    • Website
  • Upper and Lower Barrakka Gardens: Panoramic views, daily cannon firing at noon
  • Grandmaster's Palace and State Rooms (Currently under restoration, check for reopening)
  • Lascaris War Rooms: Underground WWII command center
    • Admission: €12 for adults
    • Website
  • Casa Rocca Piccola: 16th-century palace still inhabited by Maltese nobility
    • Guided tours available
    • Website
  • Republic Street: Main shopping street with cafes and historical buildings
  • National Museum of Archaeology
    • Admission: €5 for adults
    • Website
  • Manoel Theatre: One of Europe's oldest working theaters
    • Guided tours available
    • Website

Three Cities (Vittoriosa, Senglea, Cospicua)

  • Fort St. Angelo: Medieval fortress with significant role in the Great Siege of 1565
    • Admission: €8 for adults
  • Maritime Museum: Housed in former Royal Naval Bakery
  • Inquisitor's Palace: Only remaining Inquisitor's palace open to the public
  • Traditional boat ride (dghajsa) across the Grand Harbour

Mdina and Rabat

  • Mdina Old City tour (former capital, "Silent City")
  • St. Paul's Cathedral and museum
  • Palazzo Falson: Medieval townhouse museum
  • Fontanella Tea Garden: Famous for cakes and panoramic views
  • Rabat catacombs: Early Christian underground burial sites
  • Ta' Qali Crafts Village: Artisan shops, including Mdina Glass Factory Shop

Southern Malta

  • Blue Grotto boat tour
    • Boat trips: around €8 per person
  • Hagar Qim and Mnajdra Temples: UNESCO World Heritage megalithic temples
    • Admission: €10 for adults (combined ticket)
    • Website
  • Marsaxlokk fishing village and Sunday market
  • St. Peter's Pool: Natural swimming area
  • Dingli Cliffs: Highest point in Malta with scenic walks

Gozo

  • Ggantija Temples: Oldest free-standing structures in the world
    • Admission: €9 for adults
  • Cittadella in Victoria: Fortified city with panoramic views
  • Ta' Pinu Basilica: Important pilgrimage site
  • Dwejra area: Inland Sea, Fungus Rock, former Azure Window site
  • Ramla Bay: Red sand beach with Roman ruins
  • Salt pans near Marsalforn: Traditional salt harvesting site
  • Wied il-Għasri: Secluded valley and beach, popular for diving and cliff jumping
  • Tal-Mixta Cave: Offers stunning views overlooking Ramla Bay

Comino

  • Blue Lagoon: Turquoise waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling
  • Crystal Lagoon: Less crowded alternative to Blue Lagoon
  • St. Mary's Tower: 17th-century watchtower

Northern Malta

  • Popeye Village: Former film set turned tourist attraction
    • Admission: €17.95 for adults
    • Website
  • Golden Bay and Ghajn Tuffieha Bay: Popular sandy beaches
  • Mellieha Bay: Largest sandy beach in Malta
  • Red Tower (Saint Agatha's Tower): 17th-century watchtower
  • Coral Lagoon: Natural arch formation with vibrant blue waters

III. Activities and Experiences

Cultural/Historical

  • Explore ancient temples and archaeological sites
  • Visit medieval cities and fortifications
  • Tour museums and churches
  • Attend local festivals or village festas (if available)
  • Visit the Hal Saflieni Hypogeum (underground prehistoric burial site)
    • Advance booking essential
    • Website

Adventure

  • Cliff diving at Wied il-Għasri, Gozo
  • Hiking along coastal paths
  • Scuba diving or snorkeling
    • Various dive centers available, e.g., Dive Systems Malta
  • Kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding
  • Jeep tours in Gozo
  • Rock climbing and bouldering
  • Parasailing in St. George's Bay
  • Zipline (if available)
  • Coasteering: Explore Malta's rugged coastline

Relaxation

  • Beach days at sandy beaches
  • Swimming in crystal-clear waters
  • Boat tours and cruises
  • Spa treatments at luxury hotels

Culinary

  • Food tours in Valletta
  • Try pastizzi, Maltese rabbit dishes, and fresh seafood
  • Taste Cisk, the local beer
  • Wine tasting at local vineyards
  • Visit Is-Suq tal-Belt (Valletta's food market)
  • Learn to make traditional Maltese dishes in a cooking class
  • Sample ftira (Maltese bread with various toppings)
  • Try Ġbejna (traditional Maltese cheese)
  • Feast on lampuki (dorado fish) when in season (August to December)

Shopping

  • Browse for Maltese glass at Ta' Qali Crafts Village
  • Visit local markets, especially the Sunday market in Marsaxlokk
  • Shop for handmade lace in Gozo
  • Purchase local honey, jams, and olive oil

Nightlife

  • Gay bar night in St. Julian's or Paceville
  • Experience Malta's vibrant nightlife scene

V. Practical Tips

Transportation

  • Consider renting a car for flexibility (drive on the left side)
  • Public buses are available but may take longer
    • Malta Public Transport website
  • Ferries connect the main islands
    • Gozo Channel Ferry
  • Use Malta Public Transport's tallinja card for cheaper bus fares
  • Taxi apps like Bolt are available
  • Ferry to Sicily (Pozzallo): Takes about 1.5-2 hours each way
    • Potential day trip, but consider time constraints
    • Possible visits near the landing: Noto or Modica (Baroque towns)

Accommodations

  • The Embassy Valletta Hotel is recommended for luxury and location
    • Website
  • Consider staying in one base and doing day trips
  • Options range from boutique hotels to self-catering apartments

Booking

  • Book Hypogeum tickets well in advance (months ahead)
  • Use Get Your Guide for booking tours and experiences
  • Pre-book popular restaurants, especially in peak season
  • Consider the Malta Heritage Pass for access to multiple historical sites
    • Info

General

  • Visit popular sites early to avoid crowds
  • Be aware that sandy beaches are limited but special
  • Tap water is safe to drink
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring old towns
  • Best times to visit: April-June and September-October for mild weather
  • Carry cash for small purchases in villages
  • Respect dress codes when visiting churches
  • Learn a few basic Maltese phrases for a more immersive experience
  • Check event calendars for local festas and cultural events
